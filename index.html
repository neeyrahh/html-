Skin colour

Skin colour is determined by genetic inheritance but exposure to sunlight alters skin color. Melanin is a pigment that determines skin colour as well as hair and eye colour. Melanin is produced by skin cells when exposd to sun.
The more the sun exposure,the more the melanin. There are two types of melanin,eumelanin which gives skin brown colour(tan) and pheomelanin which gives skin a red colour (burn).

Regular sun exposure is associated with tanning. It increases eumelanin levels which gives skin a brownish tan and provides protection against the sun rays. Irregular sun exposure is associted with burning.
It increases pheomelanin levels and increases cancer risk. Exposure to artificial ultra-violet radiation from tanning beds has the same effect on skin colour and cancer risk as exposure to the sun ultra-violet rays.

SKIN TYPES

Skin type is a classifiction based on the skin reaction to sun exposure after a period of time. The skin reaction is measured in term of burning and taanning. The Fitzpatrick system of classifying skn type is most commonly used in the assessment of skin cancer risk. The Fitzatrick skin types are:

SKIN TYPES ATTRIBUTES
Type 1 Extrmely fair skin, always burn, never tans.
Type 2 Fair skin, always burns, sometimes tans.
Type 3 Medium skin, sometimes burns, always tans.
Type 4 Olive skin,rarely burns, always tans.
Type 5 Moderately pigmented brown skin, never burns, always tans.
Type 6 Markedly pigmented black skin, never burns,always tans.
